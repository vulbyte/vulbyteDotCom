AI POLICIES

due to the poliferation of ai, i just wanted to write a quick little tldr on my stance on ai so everyone can get be on the same page and have an idea on my stance. imma keep it pretty lightweight so don't you worry.

quick definition: when i discuss AI, i will consistently mean large models like chatGPT, google gemini, etc etc. these opinions i'm sharing largely do not apply to smaller models such as text recognition models that run on your phone or the like, so just an astarisk :3
  • AI for art what seperates an amazing brick layer who can insure every brick is laid perfectly flat vs an artist? to me an artist is someone who creates something to envoke emotion, and a piece of art that lacks that personal touch, simply is reductive from that vision. ie, a director who tells someone what to make, vs a director who directory handles the project will in my opinion always produce a better final project then whatever hands off director will make. the same is with AI, no matter how much effort or "prompting wizardry" a prompter puts into a piece of AI. TLDR: for this i personally believe AI for art outside of something very basic like interpolation or denoising should never me used.
  • AI ethics I think the way AI was trained by stealing (yes i do mean stealing) various content such as music, videos, etc are all abhorrent behavior, and their constant insisting that anything on the internet is "free", as well as platforms insistence that this is all fair play is not valid. artists should have a say where their art is used and this is blatant violation of the trusts artist put into these platforms.
  • AI for learning I personally believe given the amount of obfuscation and other issues that come with learning, i believe AI can be a good first step towards learning something. THAT BEING SAID AI SHOULD NEVER BE USED AS A FOUNDATION FOR LEARNING. AI has commonly been shown to have hallucinations and just gaslight people, so due to that i believe that AI as a "how can i learn this?" is a good step, i don't think AI for "how can i draw y" is valid and is simply a waste of everyone's time.
  • AI for early stages of a project [ie concept art] The Earliest stages of a project is where the main diversity of a project is formed and is where the "soul" of a project comes from. and this is why it is essential to never use AI at the early stages of a project
  • AI for coding AI is effectively auto complete, so as long as i'm using an ethical model, and i'm verifying the output then there's no problem, however allowing an ai to write more then say 1000-lines of code is a horrible idea and terrifys me as i have seen even the best rated AI's handle simple state management horribly.
  • AI for enhancements [ie to art] I have never had an ai "enhancement" actually improve a project. the only times i've ever tried playing with them (ie LANDR) the output has been worse in my opinion every single time.
  • AI for research AI can be used as a "word of mouth" source (tertiary) at best. it can be a starting point to look at or inquire about an idea, however due to the RNG nature of LLM's using an AI legitiment source is on the same level as trusting bathroom graffiti for life advice.
